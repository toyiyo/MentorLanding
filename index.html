From Colombia to Leading in Tech: My Journey of Resilience and Growth
Growing up in Colombia during the 80s and 90s was shaped by uncertainty and resilience.
Violence was part of daily life, but so was hope and a drive to build something better.
That drive carried me forward when I moved to the U.S. in 2000, chasing safety and opportunity.
The turning points in my journey
  • Breaking into tech as an outsider, navigating new systems and cultural barriers.
  • Transitioning from contributor to leader, where I discovered my passion for empowering teams.
  • Facing my biggest challenge: rebuilding trust and alignment within a struggling project team.
Vulnerability as a strength
At one point, I burned out, pushing too hard to "prove" myself. It wasn’t sustainable.
Vulnerability taught me to redefine success—not by endless productivity but by meaningful connections and outcomes.
The Lessons
Leadership isn’t about having all the answers—it’s about trust, collective ownership, and the courage to grow through challenges. My journey from Colombia to engineering leadership taught me that our past doesn’t define us; it equips us.
Let's talk
To those navigating their own challenges: resilience and growth often come hand in hand. What’s a lesson your journey has taught you? Let’s connect—I’d love to learn from your story.
